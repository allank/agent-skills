# Cynefin Framework

A sense-making framework by Dave Snowden for categorising problems and deciding how to respond. The name is Welsh for "habitat" or "place"—acknowledging that our decisions are shaped by our environment.

## The Five Domains

### Clear (formerly "Simple/Obvious")
- **Characteristics**: Cause and effect is obvious. Known knowns. Repeatable patterns.
- **Response**: Sense → Categorise → Respond
- **Practice**: Apply **best practice** (there is one right answer)
- **Failure mode**: Complacency; treating complex problems as clear

### Complicated
- **Characteristics**: Cause and effect is discoverable through analysis. Known unknowns. Requires expertise.
- **Response**: Sense → Analyse → Respond
- **Practice**: Apply **good practice** (multiple valid approaches, expert-dependent)
- **Failure mode**: Analysis paralysis; over-reliance on experts

### Complex
- **Characteristics**: Cause and effect only discoverable in retrospect. Unknown unknowns. Emergent patterns.
- **Response**: Probe → Sense → Respond
- **Practice**: Use **emergent practice** (safe-to-fail experiments)
- **Failure mode**: Trying to analyse or plan your way through; impatience with emergence

### Chaotic
- **Characteristics**: No discernible cause and effect. Unknowables. Crisis conditions.
- **Response**: Act → Sense → Respond
- **Practice**: Use **novel practice** (stabilise first, then assess)
- **Failure mode**: Delayed action; waiting for data that won't come

### Disorder (The Centre)
- **Characteristics**: Don't know which domain you're in.
- **Response**: Break the problem into parts and assign each to a domain
- **Failure mode**: Applying your "comfort zone" approach to everything

## Key Moves for the Thinking Partner

- **Diagnose the domain**: "What kind of problem is this? Clear, complicated, complex, or chaotic?"
- **Match the response**: "Are we trying to analyse something that needs probing? Or probing something that just needs expertise?"
- **Challenge domain drift**: "Are we treating this as complicated because we're more comfortable there, when it's actually complex?"
- **Safe-to-fail probes**: "What's a small experiment we could run to learn more about how this system behaves?"

## Boundary Dynamics

- **Cliff between Clear and Chaotic**: Complacency in Clear can lead to a sudden fall into Chaos
- **Complex → Complicated**: As patterns emerge, problems can shift rightward
- **Complicated → Complex**: New disruptions push problems back into complexity
