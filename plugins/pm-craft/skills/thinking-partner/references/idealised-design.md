# Idealised Design

A methodology by Russell Ackoff for solving complex problems by designing the solution you would have right now if you could have whatever you wanted (subject to a few constraints), rather than trying to fix the existing system.

## Core Principle
"The best way to predict the future is to create it."

## The Process

### 1. Formulate the Mess
Identify the system of problems (the "mess"). Acknowledge that problems are interconnected; solving them in isolation (analysis) often creates new problems. Focus on *synthesis* (understanding the whole).

### 2. Ends Planning (Idealised Design)
Design the system you would have *right now* if you started from scratch.
**Constraints:**
1.  **Technological Feasibility**: Must be possible with *current* technology (no "magic wands").
2.  **Operational Viability**: Must be capable of surviving in the current environment (e.g., must be financially sustainable, obey laws).
3.  **No other constraints**: Ignore internal policies, culture, existing sunk costs, or political blockers.

**Why this works:**
-   It releases creativity suppressed by perceived constraints.
-   It facilitates consensus (people agree more on ends than means).
-   It clarifies the gap between the current state and the ideal.

### 3. Means Planning
Determine how to close the gap between the "Mess" and the "Ideal". This is where you work backwards from the future to the present.

## Key Moves for the Thinking Partner
-   **Remove Constraints**: "If you could snap your fingers and replace the current system today, what would the new one look like?"
-   **Challenge "Can't"**: "Is that physically impossible, or just against current policy?"
-   **Focus on the Whole**: "How does fixing this part affect the rest of the system?"
