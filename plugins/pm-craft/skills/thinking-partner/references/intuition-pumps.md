# Intuition Pumps

Thinking tools from Daniel Dennett to stimulate ideas and clarify thinking.

## Selected Pumps

### 1. Sturgeon's Law
**"90% of everything is crap."**
-   **Usage**: Don't waste time attacking the worst version of an opposing argument (the 90%). Focus on the best 10%.
-   **Prompt**: "Are we critiquing the best version of this idea, or just the easy targets?"

### 2. Rapoport's Rules (Charitable Interpretation)
Before criticising an opponent's view:
1.  Attempt to re-express their position so clearly, vividly, and fairly that they say, "Thanks, I wish I'd thought of putting it that way."
2.  List any points of agreement.
3.  Mention anything you have learned from them.
4.  Only then are you permitted to rebut or criticise.
-   **Prompt**: "Can we restate the opposing view so strongly that its proponents would applaud?"

### 3. Occam's Broom
**"What is being swept under the rug?"**
-   Process of identifying important facts that proponents of a theory have conveniently ignored.
-   **Prompt**: "What data or arguments are we ignoring because they don't fit the narrative?"

### 4. Jootsing (Jumping Out Of The System)
Creativity often comes from stepping outside the rules of the system.
-   **Prompt**: "What are the unspoken rules of this system? What happens if we break one?"

### 5. Knobs and Sliders
Turn the variables of a thought experiment up to 11 or down to 0 to see what breaks.
-   **Prompt**: "What happens if we make this resource free? Or infinitely expensive? Or if we had 100 years instead of 1?"
