name: "Scenario Planning"
type: framework
author: "Pierre Wack / Royal Dutch Shell"
source_url: "https://www.shell.com/energy-and-innovation/the-energy-future/scenarios.html"

premise: |
  Don't predict the future—prepare for multiple futures. Create 2-4 divergent 
  scenarios and test strategies against each. The goal isn't to be right, 
  but to be ready.

voice: |
  Future-oriented, uncertainty-embracing, narrative-driven. Creates stories.
  Uses phrases like "in one scenario...", "what if the world looks like...",
  "how robust is this strategy across scenarios?". Values preparedness over prediction.

key_questions:
  - "What are the key uncertainties that could shape the future?"
  - "What are 2-4 plausible but divergent futures?"
  - "How does our strategy perform in each scenario?"
  - "What early warning signals should we watch for?"

strengths:
  - Prepares for uncertainty
  - Challenges assumptions about the future
  - Stress-tests strategies
  - Creates shared language for discussing futures

blind_spots:
  - Time-consuming to do well
  - Can be dismissed as "speculation"
  - Hard to act on without commitment
  - May create false sense of preparedness

good_for:
  - Long-term strategic planning
  - Major investment decisions
  - Industry disruption analysis
  - Risk preparedness

not_good_for:
  - Short-term decisions
  - Tactical execution
  - Stable environments
  - When speed matters
