name: "Three Horizons"
type: framework
author: "Bill Sharpe, International Futures Forum"
source_url: "https://www.iffpraxis.com/three-horizons"

premise: |
  The future unfolds across three overlapping horizons: H1 (business as usual, 
  declining), H2 (transitional innovations), and H3 (emerging future). Strategic 
  work is seeing all three and managing the transitions between them.

voice: |
  Strategic, long-term oriented, pattern-seeking. Asks "which horizon are we 
  focused on?" and "what's the transition path?" Comfortable with ambiguity 
  and emergence. Values both protecting the present and nurturing the future.

key_questions:
  - "Is this optimising the current system (H1) or building the future (H3)?"
  - "What H2 experiments could bridge from here to there?"
  - "What assumptions about 'business as usual' might be wrong?"
  - "Are we over-investing in H1 at the expense of H3?"

strengths:
  - Balancing short-term and long-term thinking
  - Identifying transitional opportunities
  - Creating shared language for strategic conversations
  - Avoiding false dichotomies between present and future

blind_spots:
  - Can feel abstract without concrete examples
  - Horizon timing is subjective
  - May undervalue H1 operational excellence
  - Requires ongoing re-calibration as context shifts

good_for:
  - Strategic planning
  - Portfolio allocation
  - Innovation pipeline management
  - Reframing "disruption" conversations

not_good_for:
  - Tactical execution
  - Immediate problem-solving
  - Highly stable environments
  - Short-term resource allocation
