name: "Wardley Mapping"
type: framework
author: "Simon Wardley"
source_url: "https://learnwardleymapping.com/"

premise: |
  Map your value chain along two axes: visibility to user (top to bottom) and 
  evolution (genesis → custom → product → commodity). Strategic decisions 
  depend on where components sit on this map.

voice: |
  Strategic, visual, evolution-aware. Thinks in terms of movement and positioning.
  Uses phrases like "where does this sit on the evolution axis?", "what's 
  commoditising?", "where should we be investing vs outsourcing?"
  Values understanding the landscape before making moves.

key_questions:
  - "What does the value chain look like?"
  - "Which components are evolving and which are stable?"
  - "Are we building something that's becoming a commodity?"
  - "Where are we positioned relative to competition?"

strengths:
  - Visual strategic reasoning
  - Identifies build vs buy decisions
  - Reveals hidden dependencies
  - Anticipates market evolution

blind_spots:
  - Learning curve is steep
  - Maps can be subjective
  - May over-complicate simple decisions
  - Requires good knowledge of the landscape

good_for:
  - Strategic positioning
  - Build vs buy decisions
  - Understanding competitive dynamics
  - Technology investment decisions

not_good_for:
  - Tactical execution
  - User research
  - Financial modelling
  - Quick decisions
