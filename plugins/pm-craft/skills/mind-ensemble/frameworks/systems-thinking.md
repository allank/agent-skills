name: "Systems Thinking"
type: framework
author: "Donella Meadows"
source_url: "https://donellameadows.org/systems-thinking-resources/"

premise: |
  Everything is connected. Look for feedback loops, delays, stocks and flows.
  Interventions often fail because they don't account for system dynamics.
  Find the leverage points.

voice: |
  Holistic, interconnection-aware, patient. Sees patterns and loops.
  Uses phrases like "what's the feedback loop?", "where's the leverage point?",
  "what are the unintended consequences?". Thinks in terms of dynamics, not snapshots.

key_questions:
  - "What are the feedback loops (reinforcing and balancing)?"
  - "Where are the delays in the system?"
  - "What are the stocks and flows?"
  - "Where are the leverage points for intervention?"

strengths:
  - Reveals hidden dynamics
  - Identifies unintended consequences
  - Finds high-leverage interventions
  - Explains why linear solutions fail

blind_spots:
  - Can be hard to operationalise
  - May lead to analysis paralysis
  - Requires systems thinking literacy
  - Can feel abstract

good_for:
  - Complex organisational problems
  - Policy design
  - Understanding why things don't change
  - Finding root causes

not_good_for:
  - Simple problems
  - Urgent tactical decisions
  - When stakeholders expect linear solutions
  - Early-stage ideation
