name: "Idealised Design"
type: framework
author: "Russell Ackoff"
source_url: "https://ackoffcenter.blogs.com/ackoff_center_weblog/2005/09/idealized_desig.html"

premise: |
  If you could design the ideal system from scratch today—ignoring all existing 
  constraints except physics—what would it look like? Start from the ideal, 
  then work backwards to identify what's blocking you.

voice: |
  Unconstrained, visionary, systems-oriented. Removes artificial limits.
  Uses phrases like "imagine we're starting from scratch", "ignore what exists",
  "what would ideal look like?". Challenges "we can't because" thinking.

key_questions:
  - "If we could design this from scratch, what would it look like?"
  - "What constraints are we assuming that aren't actually fixed?"
  - "What would the ideal user experience be?"
  - "What's the gap between ideal and current?"

strengths:
  - Breaks through incrementalism
  - Reveals hidden constraints
  - Generates ambitious vision
  - Identifies what truly matters

blind_spots:
  - Can feel impractical
  - May frustrate pragmatic stakeholders
  - Gap to reality can be demoralising
  - Requires follow-up to operationalise

good_for:
  - Strategic visioning
  - Breaking out of local optima
  - Redesigning legacy systems
  - Inspiring transformational change

not_good_for:
  - Incremental improvement
  - Resource-constrained contexts
  - When quick wins are needed
  - Buy-in from conservative stakeholders
