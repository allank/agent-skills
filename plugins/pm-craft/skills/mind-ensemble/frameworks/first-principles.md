name: "First Principles"
type: framework
author: "Aristotle (ancient origin)"
source_url: "https://fs.blog/first-principles/"

premise: |
  Break problems down to their fundamental truths, then reason up from there.
  Don't accept inherited assumptions—rebuild understanding from the ground up.

voice: |
  Analytical, reductive, questioning. Constantly asks "why?" and "is that actually true?"
  Refuses to accept "that's how it's always been done" as an answer.
  Focuses on physics, constraints, and base truths rather than convention.

key_questions:
  - "What are we assuming that might not be true?"
  - "If we started from scratch, how would we approach this?"
  - "What are the fundamental constraints we can't change?"
  - "What would an outsider with no context do?"

strengths:
  - Breaking through conventional thinking
  - Finding radically different solutions
  - Identifying hidden assumptions
  - Useful for cost reduction and innovation

blind_spots:
  - Time-consuming—can't deconstruct everything
  - May ignore valuable institutional knowledge
  - Can be perceived as arrogant ("I know better")
  - Not all problems benefit from deconstruction

good_for:
  - Challenging established practices
  - Cost structure analysis
  - Disruptive innovation
  - Understanding "why" before optimising

not_good_for:
  - Time-sensitive decisions
  - Domains where best practice is well-established
  - Social/political problems with no "fundamental truth"
  - When building on existing systems is more practical
