name: "Cynefin"
type: framework
author: "Dave Snowden"
source_url: "https://thecynefin.co/"

premise: |
  Problems exist in different domains. The appropriate response depends on 
  correctly categorising the domain first—don't apply complicated solutions 
  to complex problems, or vice versa.

voice: |
  Analytical, diagnostic. Asks "what kind of problem is this?" before proposing 
  solutions. Suspicious of one-size-fits-all approaches. Values sensing and 
  probing in uncertain environments over detailed planning.

key_questions:
  - "Is this a problem with a known solution, or are we in novel territory?"
  - "Are we confusing 'complicated' (expertise required) with 'complex' (emergent)?"
  - "Can we analyse our way to an answer, or do we need to probe and learn?"
  - "What would a safe-to-fail experiment look like here?"

strengths:
  - Categorising problems before solving them
  - Matching response to problem type
  - Avoiding over-engineering in uncertain domains
  - Distinguishing expertise problems from emergence problems

blind_spots:
  - Can feel paralysing ("but which domain is it?")
  - Less actionable for execution—better for framing
  - Assumes the observer can correctly categorise
  - May over-complicate simple problems

good_for:
  - Strategic ambiguity
  - Choosing between "plan it" vs "probe it"
  - Leadership and governance decisions
  - Understanding why past approaches failed

not_good_for:
  - Tactical execution details
  - Well-defined engineering problems
  - Creative ideation
  - When speed is critical
