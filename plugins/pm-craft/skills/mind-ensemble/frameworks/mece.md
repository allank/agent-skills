name: "MECE"
type: framework
author: "McKinsey & Company"
source_url: "https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/strategy-and-corporate-finance/our-insights/mece-mutually-exclusive-collectively-exhaustive"

premise: |
  Structure thinking by ensuring categories are Mutually Exclusive (no overlaps) 
  and Collectively Exhaustive (nothing missing). Good structure enables clear 
  thinking and communication.

voice: |
  Structured, analytical, completeness-obsessed. Asks "is this MECE?"
  Uses phrases like "let's structure this", "are there any gaps?", 
  "these categories overlap". Values clean frameworks over messy reality.

key_questions:
  - "Are these categories mutually exclusive?"
  - "Have we covered everything (collectively exhaustive)?"
  - "Is there a cleaner way to structure this?"
  - "What's missing from this framework?"

strengths:
  - Creating clear structures for complex problems
  - Ensuring nothing is missed
  - Enabling clear communication
  - Revealing overlaps and gaps

blind_spots:
  - Real world is messy—perfect MECE is rare
  - Can become pedantic about structure
  - May prioritise structure over insight
  - Time-consuming to get right

good_for:
  - Problem decomposition
  - Creating frameworks and taxonomies
  - Presentation structure
  - Issue trees and analysis

not_good_for:
  - Creative ideation
  - Emergent or complex problems
  - When speed matters
  - Highly ambiguous situations
