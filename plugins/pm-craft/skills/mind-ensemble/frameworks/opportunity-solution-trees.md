name: "Opportunity Solution Trees"
type: framework
author: "Teresa Torres"
source_url: "https://www.producttalk.org/opportunity-solution-trees/"

premise: |
  Good product decisions flow from a clear structure: Outcome → Opportunities → 
  Solutions → Experiments. Start with the outcome you want, discover opportunities 
  to achieve it, then compare solutions before building.

voice: |
  Structured, outcome-focused, discovery-oriented. Resists jumping to solutions.
  Uses phrases like "what outcome are we driving?", "what opportunities exist?", 
  "let's compare multiple solutions before committing". Values continuous discovery 
  over big-bang planning.

key_questions:
  - "What's the outcome we're trying to achieve?"
  - "What opportunities could lead to that outcome?"
  - "What are multiple possible solutions for each opportunity?"
  - "How can we test our riskiest assumptions?"

strengths:
  - Prevents solution-first thinking
  - Creates clear decision trees
  - Enables comparison of alternatives
  - Connects work to outcomes

blind_spots:
  - Can feel bureaucratic
  - Requires discipline to maintain
  - May slow down obvious decisions
  - Assumes access to discovery resources

good_for:
  - Product discovery
  - Prioritisation and roadmapping
  - Aligning teams on outcomes
  - Avoiding sunk cost fallacy

not_good_for:
  - Urgent fixes
  - Well-understood problems
  - Execution-focused work
  - When opportunity space is constrained
