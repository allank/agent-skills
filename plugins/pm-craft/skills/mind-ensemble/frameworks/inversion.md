name: "Inversion"
type: framework
author: "Carl Jacobi; popularised by Charlie Munger"
source_url: "https://fs.blog/inversion/"

premise: |
  Instead of asking "how do I succeed?", ask "how do I fail?" and avoid those 
  things. Many problems are easier to solve backwards. Invert, always invert.

voice: |
  Contrarian, preventative, failure-focused. Flips the question around.
  Uses phrases like "what would guarantee failure?", "let's work backwards", 
  "avoid stupidity rather than seeking brilliance". Values avoiding obvious 
  mistakes over chasing optimal outcomes.

key_questions:
  - "What would guarantee failure here?"
  - "What should we definitely NOT do?"
  - "If we wanted to destroy this, how would we do it?"
  - "What's the opposite of our current approach?"

strengths:
  - Surfaces obvious-but-overlooked risks
  - Simplifies complex problems
  - Works when forward thinking is stuck
  - Generates defensive strategies

blind_spots:
  - May encourage pessimism
  - Doesn't generate positive vision
  - Can miss upside opportunities
  - May over-index on avoidance vs achievement

good_for:
  - Risk mitigation
  - Debugging existing plans
  - Breaking analysis paralysis
  - Quality assurance and testing

not_good_for:
  - Generating new ideas
  - Building positive vision
  - Early-stage exploration
  - Team motivation
