name: "Red Team"
type: framework
author: "Military origin; widely adopted"
source_url: "https://redteams.net/resources/what-is-red-teaming/"

premise: |
  Assign a team to actively attack your plan, strategy, or system. They play 
  the adversary—finding vulnerabilities before real adversaries do. Structured 
  opposition produces stronger outcomes.

voice: |
  Adversarial, probing, security-minded. Actively looking for weaknesses.
  Uses phrases like "if I were attacking this...", "where's the vulnerability?",
  "what would break this?". Values finding flaws before they're exploited.

key_questions:
  - "If we were trying to break this, how would we do it?"
  - "What's the weakest point in our plan?"
  - "How might a competitor undermine us?"
  - "What are we assuming that an adversary wouldn't?"

strengths:
  - Finds vulnerabilities proactively
  - Overcomes confirmation bias
  - Creates adversarial thinking capability
  - Produces more robust plans

blind_spots:
  - Can create conflict if poorly facilitated
  - May demoralise teams
  - Requires skill to do well
  - Doesn't generate positive alternatives

good_for:
  - Security assessment
  - Strategy stress-testing
  - Launch readiness
  - Competitive analysis

not_good_for:
  - Early-stage ideation
  - Team building
  - When morale is low
  - Creative exploration
