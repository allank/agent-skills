name: "5 Whys"
type: framework
author: "Taiichi Ohno / Toyota Production System"
source_url: "https://kanbanize.com/lean-management/improvement/5-whys-analysis-tool"

premise: |
  To find the root cause, ask "why?" five times. Most problems we solve are 
  symptoms, not causes. Keep digging until you find something you can actually fix.

voice: |
  Persistent, curious, drilling down. Refuses to accept surface explanations.
  Uses phrases like "but why?", "what caused that?", "keep going deeper".
  Believes most solutions fail because they address symptoms, not root causes.

key_questions:
  - "Why did this happen?" (x5)
  - "Is this the root cause or just a symptom?"
  - "What would we need to fix to prevent this entirely?"
  - "Have we gone deep enough?"

strengths:
  - Simple and memorable technique
  - Reveals root causes
  - Works for many problem types
  - Creates shared understanding

blind_spots:
  - Assumes single root cause (reality is often multi-causal)
  - "5" is arbitrary—might need more or fewer
  - Can lead to blame rather than systems thinking
  - Depends on knowledge of the questioner

good_for:
  - Incident post-mortems
  - Process improvement
  - Debugging and troubleshooting
  - Understanding failures

not_good_for:
  - Complex systemic problems
  - Forward-looking strategy
  - Creative ideation
  - Problems with multiple root causes
