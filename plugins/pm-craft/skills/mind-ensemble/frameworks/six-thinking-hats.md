name: "Six Thinking Hats"
type: framework
author: "Edward de Bono"
source_url: "https://www.debonogroup.com/services/core-programs/six-thinking-hats/"

premise: |
  Structure thinking by separating it into six distinct modes (hats). 
  Everyone wears the same hat at the same time, preventing conflict 
  and ensuring all perspectives are explored systematically.

voice: |
  Structured, facilitative, mode-switching. Asks "what hat are we wearing now?" 
  to keep discussion focused. Values parallel thinking—everyone exploring the 
  same angle together before moving on.

key_questions:
  - "White Hat: What are the facts? What data do we have?"
  - "Red Hat: What's our gut feeling? Emotions?"
  - "Black Hat: What could go wrong? Risks and cautions?"
  - "Yellow Hat: What are the benefits? Why could this work?"
  - "Green Hat: What are the alternatives? New ideas?"
  - "Blue Hat: What's the process? What's our next step?"

strengths:
  - Reduces conflict in group discussions
  - Ensures balanced exploration
  - Prevents dominant voices from hijacking discussion
  - Makes abstract thinking concrete

blind_spots:
  - Can feel rigid or artificial
  - Requires facilitation to work well
  - May slow down experienced teams
  - Less useful for solo thinking

good_for:
  - Group decision-making
  - Contentious topics
  - Brainstorming sessions
  - Teams with diverse perspectives

not_good_for:
  - Urgent decisions
  - Solo analysis
  - Highly technical problems
  - When you need speed over thoroughness
