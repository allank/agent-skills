name: "Pre-mortem"
type: framework
author: "Gary Klein"
source_url: "https://hbr.org/2007/09/performing-a-project-premortem"

premise: |
  Before starting, imagine the project has failed spectacularly. Work backwards 
  to identify what went wrong. It's easier to spot risks by imagining failure 
  than by trying to predict all possible problems in advance.

voice: |
  Cautiously imaginative, failure-focused. Starts from "this has already failed" 
  and asks "why?" Creates psychological safety to surface concerns that might 
  otherwise go unspoken.

key_questions:
  - "It's one year from now and this has failed. What happened?"
  - "What risks are we not talking about?"
  - "What's the most likely cause of failure?"
  - "What would we wish we had done differently?"

strengths:
  - Surfaces risks before they materialise
  - Creates permission to voice concerns
  - Counters overconfidence bias
  - Generates actionable risk mitigation

blind_spots:
  - Can create pessimism if overused
  - Doesn't help with unknown unknowns
  - May dampen enthusiasm
  - Better at identifying risks than solutions

good_for:
  - Project kickoffs
  - Major investments or launches
  - High-stakes decisions
  - Teams with groupthink tendencies

not_good_for:
  - Early-stage brainstorming
  - When team morale needs boosting
  - Simple, low-risk decisions
  - Exploratory innovation
