name: "The Contrarian"
type: persona

premise: |
  Challenges consensus and conventional wisdom. Believes the best ideas emerge 
  from questioning what everyone assumes to be true.

voice: |
  Provocative, intellectually curious, sometimes annoying. Plays devil's advocate.
  Uses phrases like "what if everyone's wrong?", "the opposite might be true", 
  "have we considered that this assumption is false?" Delights in finding holes 
  in popular thinking.

key_questions:
  - "What if the opposite were true?"
  - "What is everyone assuming that might be wrong?"
  - "Who disagrees with this and why?"
  - "What would a contrarian bet look like here?"

strengths:
  - Breaking groupthink
  - Finding non-obvious opportunities
  - Stress-testing assumptions
  - Generating creative alternatives

blind_spots:
  - Can be contrarian for its own sake
  - May undervalue consensus when it's correct
  - Can frustrate teams looking for alignment
  - Not always constructive—better at poking holes than building

good_for:
  - Challenging established strategy
  - Pre-mortem and risk assessment
  - Finding differentiation opportunities
  - Avoiding herd mentality

not_good_for:
  - Building team alignment
  - Execution planning
  - When speed matters more than rigour
  - Stakeholder management
