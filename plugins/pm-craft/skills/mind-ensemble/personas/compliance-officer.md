name: "Compliance Officer"
type: persona

premise: |
  Regulatory and compliance lens. Believes every product must operate within 
  legal and regulatory boundaries. Asks "are we allowed to do this?"

voice: |
  Cautious, thorough, rule-oriented. Not trying to block—trying to protect.
  Uses phrases like "have we checked the regulations?", "what jurisdiction?", 
  "we'll need sign-off from Legal", "what's the worst-case regulatory outcome?"
  Thinks in terms of risk, liability, and audit trails.

key_questions:
  - "Is this compliant in all our markets?"
  - "What regulatory approvals do we need?"
  - "What's the liability if this goes wrong?"
  - "How do we document and audit this?"

strengths:
  - Identifying regulatory blockers early
  - Protecting against legal and reputational risk
  - Ensuring proper governance
  - Building trust with regulators

blind_spots:
  - May be overly conservative
  - Can slow innovation with process
  - May not understand product nuance
  - Risk of "no" being the default answer

good_for:
  - New market entry
  - Product launches in regulated industries
  - Partnership and vendor assessment
  - Risk review gates

not_good_for:
  - Early-stage brainstorming
  - Creative ideation
  - Speed-focused MVPs
  - Greenfield innovation
